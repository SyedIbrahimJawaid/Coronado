# Content Strategy: Outranking Coronado Competitors

## Overview

Winning in Coronado requires **information-gain pages**: locally specific, data-backed, and regularly refreshed. Avoid generic lifestyle filler. Every indexable page must add something competitors do not.

## Authority Pillars

### 1. Coronado Hub Page (`/coronado/`)
**Purpose:** Index of neighborhoods, micro-markets, and market report highlights

**Content Requirements:**
- Comprehensive neighborhood index
- Links to all micro-markets (Shores, Cays, Village, etc.)
- Market report highlights and key metrics
- Fast paths to all property types
- Internal linking structure

**Update Frequency:** Quarterly

**Publishing Checklist:**

---

### 3. Neighborhood Guides (20-60 pages)
**Purpose:** Strict template with unique local intel

**Template Requirements:**
- **Intro:** 90-140 words localized summary
- **Local Intel:** 8-15 specific local details:
  - Specific streets and their character
  - Pocket feel descriptions
  - Beach access points (exact locations)
  - Parking patterns (street-by-street notes)
  - School notes (proximity, quality)
  - Shopping clusters (specific stores/areas)
  - Traffic patterns
  - Noise levels
  - Walkability scores
  - Public transit access
- **Highlights:** Best for, housing types, parking, beach access, HOA notes
- **Market Context:** Price range with disclaimer, typical DOM
- **Pros & Cons:** Balanced assessment
- **FAQs:** 3-5 neighborhood-specific questions

**Information Gain Guidelines:**
- Include details competitors don't have
- Use specific street names and locations
- Mention exact beach access points
- Note parking challenges/solutions
- Reference local businesses and amenities
- Include school proximity and quality notes

**Update Frequency:** Quarterly for top neighborhoods, annually for others

---

## Information Gain Guidelines

### Neighborhood Pages Must Include:

1. **8-15 Specific Local Details:**
   - Street names and their character
   - Pocket feel descriptions (e.g., "The blocks between Orange and C avenues feel quieter")
   - Exact beach access points (e.g., "Access via Ocean Boulevard at 1st Street")
   - Parking patterns (e.g., "Street parking is tight after 5pm, but driveways are common")
   - School notes (e.g., "Coronado Elementary is a 5-minute walk from most Village homes")
   - Shopping clusters (e.g., "Orange Avenue between 8th and 10th has the best local shops")
   - Traffic patterns (e.g., "Morning commute to downtown is 15 minutes via bridge")
   - Noise levels (e.g., "Quieter than Shores, but some street noise on main arteries")
   - Walkability (e.g., "Walk Score: 92 - most errands can be done on foot")
   - Public transit (e.g., "Bus stops on Orange Avenue every 15 minutes")

2. **Balanced Pros & Cons:**
   - Pros: What makes the neighborhood great
   - Cons: Honest assessment of challenges
   - Avoid generic statements

3. **Market Context:**
   - Price range with disclaimer
   - Typical days on market
   - Link to market report

4. **Unique Local Intel:**
   - Information competitors don't have
   - Specific, actionable details
   - Data-backed insights

## Content Quality Checklist

Before publishing any page, ensure:

- [ ] **Information Gain:** Does this add something competitors don't have?
- [ ] **Local Specificity:** Are there specific streets, locations, or details?
- [ ] **Data-Backed:** Are claims supported by data or local knowledge?
- [ ] **Balanced:** Does it include pros and cons?
- [ ] **Actionable:** Can readers use this information to make decisions?
- [ ] **Fresh:** Is the content current and regularly updated?
- [ ] **No Generic Filler:** Avoid generic lifestyle content
